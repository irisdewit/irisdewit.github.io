    About Me

    As an open-minded and curious individual, I tend to be in the middle of many different fields and interests. One of these fields is the intersection of psychology and technology. My journey in psychology and technology has equipped me with a deep understanding of human behavior and the interaction with technology.
    My commitment lies more and more in applying this knowledge to build a world where sustainability and social responsibility take center stage. I'm not content with simply designing interfaces or crafting user experiences; I aspire to apply my expertise to contribute positively to society, preferably working together with great people. My ultimate goal is to use technology as a force for good, propelling us toward a more sustainable and inclusive future. I would like to work in projects that embody these values, and contribute in a team by applying my skills from user-research to prototyping to front-end design.

    During my bachelor's program in Psychology & Technology with a focus on robotics, I developed a strong foundation in understanding human behavior and cognition. Also, the choices in design are based on the human's behavior and cognition. I learned how technology influences people and how you can let technology work for people by using psychological knowledge. This knowledge became invaluable for creating user-centered design solutions in UX.



    In my double master's program at the European Institute of Technology (EIT), I am focusing on user interface and experience design, with a strong emphasis on core principles like usability, user-centric design, and prototyping. I specialize in mobile app design, from user research and low-fidelity prototyping to high-fidelity UI/UX and front-end development. Additionally, my minor in Innovation and Entrepreneurship allows me to apply these principles effectively in real-world contexts, bridging the gap between theory and practical implementation in innovative and entrepreneurial ventures.
